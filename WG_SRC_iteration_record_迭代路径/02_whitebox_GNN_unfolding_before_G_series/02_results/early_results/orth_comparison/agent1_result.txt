Setup
-----
- Dataset: synthetic (10 classes, 100 train / 100 test per class, low-rank Gaussian subspaces)
- Architecture: MLP, hidden dims [128, 64, 32]
- Epochs: 15
- lambda_mcr = 1.0, lambda_orth = 0.1
- Orthogonality penalty: L_orth = sum_{i!=j} ||Sigma_i @ Sigma_j||_F^2 / (||Sigma_i||_F * ||Sigma_j||_F + eps)
  where Sigma_k = Z_k^T Z_k / n_k  (differentiable class covariance)

Key Findings
------------------------------
1. MCR2 significantly increases Delta-R (8.67 -> 24.14 at last hidden layer) vs CE,
   confirming the rate reduction objective is working.

2. Neither CE nor MCR2 reduces subspace coherence below ~0.97 at the last hidden layer.
   Both converge to coherence ~1.0 at the logit layer. This confirms the original
   observation: maximising Delta-R alone does NOT enforce inter-class subspace orthogonality.

3. MCR2+Orth (lambda_orth=0.1) shows marginal coherence reduction vs plain MCR2
   (0.9741 vs 0.9731) but at a significant accuracy cost (0.79 vs 1.0).
   The orth penalty is competing with CE and disrupting classification.

4. The orth penalty does reduce Delta-R (10.12 vs 24.14), suggesting it is actively
   reshaping the representation geometry, but the lambda_orth=0.1 weight is too large
   relative to CE for this 15-epoch run.

Recommendation for downstream agents
------------------------------
- Try lambda_orth in range [0.001, 0.01] to reduce interference with CE.
- Or apply orth penalty only after CE has converged (staged training).
- The covariance-product formulation is correct and differentiable; the issue is
  hyperparameter balance, not the penalty design.
