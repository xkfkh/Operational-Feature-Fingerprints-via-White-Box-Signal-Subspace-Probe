Whitebox GCN (CRATE-style) - Agent 4 Results
============================================================

Model: WhiteboxGCNCRATE
Dataset: Cora (semi-supervised node classification)

Design
----------------------------------------
Each layer = GraphSmoothCompress + ISTASparsify
  Step 1: Z_half = Z + eta * A_norm @ Z @ (U U^T)
  Step 2: Z_out  = ReLU(Z_half + eta*(Z_half - Z_half @ D^T D) - eta*lam)
Loss = CE - lambda_mcr*DeltaR + lambda_sparse*||Z||_1

----------------------------------------
  Config:   run4_3layers
  Val acc:  0.7600
  Test acc: 0.7510

Key findings
----------------------------------------
1. Graph-smooth compression (Step 1) acts as a structured low-rank
   projection guided by the learned subspace U, analogous to CRATE attention.
2. ISTA sparsification (Step 2) promotes sparse activations in the
   dictionary basis D, analogous to CRATE MLP/ISTA.
3. MCR2 DeltaR loss (on training nodes) encourages class-discriminative
   representations consistent with the rate-reduction objective.
4. The whitebox interpretation provides a principled optimization
   objective for each layer operation, unlike standard black-box GCN.
5. Learnable step sizes (log-parameterized eta, lam) allow per-layer
   adaptation of compression and sparsification strength.
